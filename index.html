Biography

A native of upstate New York, Kathryn earned her bachelor’s degree in Music Education with a performer’s certificate from the Crane School of Music at SUNY Potsdam. While there, she performed with the Orchestra of Northern New York and the Northern Symphonic Winds, in addition to her university ensembles.

She completed her graduate studies in horn performance at Michigan State University in 2009, and was an active freelancer throughout mid-Michigan, performing in symphony orchestras, ballet orchestras, and chamber ensembles. She was also the adjunct horn and trumpet professor at Spring Arbor University while in graduate school. Throughout her playing career, Ms. Svatek has had the honor of performing at venues such as Carnegie Hall, Ozawa Hall at Tanglewood, and the Troy Savings Bank Music Hall.

She now co-owns Rural Soul Studio, a private music education facility in Greenwich, NY, and lives in Colonie, NY with her husband and their family.

French Horn

Kathryn Svatek is currently a freelance horn player in the greater Capital Region and a founding member of Velocity Horns, upstate New York’s progressive horn quartet. She is principal horn in the Sage City Symphony, and regularly performs with the Albany Pro Musica Orchestra, Burnt Hills Oratorio, Octavo Singers, Capital Region Wind Ensemble, and the Schenectady Symphony Orchestra. As a chamber musician, Kathryn has been a part of the Tuesdays at Noon concert series at St. Paul’s Church in Troy, and has also played for the Tasting Notes and Classical Breakfast fundraisers for the Schenectady Symphony Orchestra.
